FontMLLM: A Multimodal LLM for Open-Set Font Recognition via Augmented Synthetic Similarity

Code Dataset

Abstract

Identifying fonts in artwork is a critical step in graphic design, which is fundamental to copyright protection and artistic creation. However, existing Visual Font Recognition (VFR) models rely on classification approaches, which are unsuitable for open-set fonts as font categories grow. Besides, these models are trained on monochrome text images, leading to a significant domain gap with real-world data.

To address these challenges, we propose FontMLLM, a novel architecture that employs learnable queries to leverage the text recognition and image understanding capabilities of Multimodal Large Language Models for VFR. Moreover, we introduce a training-free similarity-based approach via an augmented synthetic image to effectively identify open-set fonts. Furthermore, to bridge the domain gap between training data and real-world data, FontMLLM provides a refined segmentation prompt that enables the Segment Anything Model to generate precise mask images, minimizing interference from outlines, colors, and backgrounds. These designs enable FontMLLM to perform end-to-end segmentation-based VFR, highlighting its potential as a unified model for text-related tasks. Moreover, we present the first large-scale multilingual VFR dataset, named DesignVFR. Our method achieves Top-5 accuracy of 95.62\% on $\text{Poster}_{real}$ and 87.04\% on $\text{Video}_{real}$ for close-set VFR, while reaching 92.74\% and 85.95\% for open-set VFR, respectively.
